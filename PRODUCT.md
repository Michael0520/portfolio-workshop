## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors arriving to evaluate one person: recruiters and hiring managers
skimming for fit in under a minute, fellow engineers checking depth, and the
occasional potential collaborator or client. They land from a CV link, a
LinkedIn/GitHub profile, or a conference badge — usually on mobile, often with
several other tabs open. The job to be done: decide quickly "is this person
worth a conversation?" and find the one link (email, GitHub, the standout
project) that lets them act on it.

## Product Purpose

A single-page personal site that presents who someone is, what they've built,
and how to reach them — fast and credibly. It exists to convert a cold visitor
into a warm contact. Success is a visitor who, within one screen, understands
the person's focus, trusts the craft on display, and clicks through to a
project, repo, or the contact link. The design *is* the product: there is
nothing to sell beyond the impression the page itself makes.

## Brand Personality

Tech-minimal and quietly confident. Voice is plain and direct — short
sentences, no hype, specifics over adjectives. Three words: **confident,
clear, understated**. The page should feel like a capable engineer who lets
the work speak: calm whitespace, restrained motion, every element earning its
place. Emotional goal — the visitor feels they've met someone competent and
trustworthy, not someone performing.

## Anti-references

- **Bought/templated source-code-sale look**: generic "buy this theme" energy,
  identical hero-over-gradient everyone has seen, stock-photo filler.
- **Buzzword soup**: "passionate ninja rockstar leveraging synergies." Say what
  was actually built and for whom.
- **Fake social proof**: invented testimonials, logo walls of companies the
  person never worked with, vanity metrics.
- **Over-animation**: motion as decoration that delays reading. Animation here
  serves entrance and focus, never spectacle.

## Design Principles

1. **Let the work speak.** Projects and real contributions carry the page;
   copy frames them, it doesn't oversell them.
2. **One screen, one decision.** A first-time visitor should grasp the person
   and find their next click without scrolling far or hunting.
3. **Earned restraint.** Whitespace, a single accent, and quiet typography read
   as confidence — not as a missing design.
4. **Specifics over adjectives.** Concrete projects, roles, and outcomes beat
   "experienced" and "results-driven."
5. **Honest by default.** Nothing on the page implies more than is true; trust
   is the whole asset.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA** as the default. Maintain AA text contrast in both light
and dark themes (the site ships with both). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` —
the entrance animations (BlurFade) and the background FlickeringGrid should
quiet down or stop for users who opt out. Ensure all interactive elements
(nav dock, social links, project links, theme toggle) are keyboard-reachable
with visible focus states, and that link/button labels read sensibly to a
screen reader. These are sensible defaults for a public personal site; adjust
if you have specific audience needs.
